# Validation

Phase 1 validation is limited to bootstrap checks:

- package imports;
- configuration validation;
- run-manifest creation;
- formatting, linting, typing, and tests.

## Phase 2

Phase 2 validation covers Kerr metric, horizon, ISCO, circular equatorial
orbits, and the zero-torque Novikov-Thorne efficiency proxy `1 - E_ISCO`.

Implemented checks:

- mandatory ISCO benchmark table from `KERR_DISK_CODEX_RESEARCH_GUIDE.md`;
- mandatory efficiency benchmark table from the guide;
- Schwarzschild-limit metric components;
- `g_mu_alpha g^alpha_nu = delta_mu^nu` residual checks;
- finite metric derivatives outside the horizon;
- domain errors for invalid spin, invalid coordinates, horizon singularities,
  and radii inside the ISCO;
- Hypothesis checks over the declared spin domain and radii outside the horizon
  or ISCO.

Phase 2 does not validate disk flux, spectra, ray tracing, synthetic data, or
inference.

## Phase 4

Phase 4 validates local thermal emission and color-correction models.

Implemented checks:

- effective-temperature round trip with `sigma_SB T^4`;
- numerical integration of `B_nu` against `sigma_SB T^4 / pi`;
- diluted-blackbody bolometric flux preservation for several `f_col` values;
- no runtime warnings over broad frequency and temperature ranges;
- explicit validation errors for invalid frequency, temperature, intensity,
  angular cosine, and color-correction inputs;
- visible clipping status for luminosity-dependent `f_col` laws.

Phase 4 keeps the angular emission isotropic. Limb darkening is not enabled.

## Phase 5

Phase 5 validates the scalar reference ray tracer.

Implemented checks:

- static-observer tetrad orthonormality;
- initial photon null constraint;
- Hamiltonian RHS conservation of `p_t` and `p_phi`;
- RK4 single-step null preservation for a simple ray;
- invariant drift for an escaping Schwarzschild ray;
- Schwarzschild shadow transition bracket around `sqrt(27)`;
- disk-crossing event detection and event residual;
- explicit `HORIZON_CAPTURE`, `ESCAPED`, `DISK_HIT`, `MAX_STEPS`, and
  `NUMERICAL_FAILURE` outcomes;
- input-validation tests for observer, screen, disk, and integration settings.

Current limitations:

- the observer screen uses a documented large-radius local tetrad convention;
- disk-crossing root refinement is linear between accepted RK4 states;
- this is a scalar correctness reference, not an optimized production image
  tracer;
- transfer-map quantities, redshift factors, and observed spectra begin in
  Phase 6.

## Phase 8

Phase 8 adds the controlled Agol-Krolik inner-edge stress extension to the
Page-Thorne disk flux. The implemented model uses the additive flux term from
Agol and Krolik (2000), equation (8), parameterized by an added efficiency
`Delta_eta`.

Implemented checks:

- `Delta_eta = 0` exactly recovers the Page-Thorne baseline flux array;
- the added stress flux is nonnegative over the adopted spin/radius domain;
- the integrated added luminosity at infinity matches `mdot Delta_eta`;
- the total stressed luminosity equals the zero-torque luminosity plus the
  added efficiency increment;
- the large-radius stress-flux slope approaches `r^(-7/2)`;
- invalid `Delta_eta` values, including negative, nonfinite, and values above
  the declared controlled range, are rejected.

Current limitations:

- This is a time-steady thin-disk stress prescription, not a GRMHD simulation.
- The implementation does not include returning radiation, photon capture,
  plunging-region emission, or a radially distributed torque inside the ISCO.
- The controlled production domain is `0 <= Delta_eta <= 0.1`; larger stress
  excursions require a new documented source and validation decision.

## Phase 9

Phase 9 adds deterministic detector-independent synthetic spectra and matched
likelihoods for Gaussian debug noise and Poisson count noise.

Implemented checks:

- logarithmic energy-bin centers and widths follow the registered convention;
- photon-flux-density integration uses the midpoint rule;
- hierarchical seed derivation is deterministic and replayable;
- multi-epoch datasets store distinct epoch/noise seeds and truth metadata;
- Gaussian relative-error noise has sample mean and variance consistent with
  theory over repeated deterministic seeds;
- Poisson count noise has sample mean and variance consistent with theory;
- Gaussian and Poisson log-likelihoods match manual formulas;
- invalid model expectations return `-inf` instead of propagating NaN;
- likelihoods are finite over a tested valid scalar grid;
- a high-signal same-model scalar injection is recovered with the bounded
  likelihood maximizer.

Current limitations:

- Phase 9 uses scalar effective area and exposure only; there is no detector
  redistribution matrix, background model, calibration uncertainty, or
  response-file handling.
- The same-model recovery test is a high-signal scalar check, not a posterior
  coverage or sampler validation.
- Production sampler selection and full relativistic-model SBC remain Phase 10+
  validation work.

## Phase 10

Phase 10 adds the first inference and calibration layer.

Implemented checks:

- bounded uniform priors transform correctly between unit-cube and physical
  coordinates;
- prior log density rejects parameters outside declared bounds with `-inf`;
- deterministic coordinate-pattern optimization recovers the mode of a
  one-dimensional Gaussian target;
- invalid optimizer and sampler controls raise explicit errors;
- the random-walk Metropolis adapter runs repeated chains on a Gaussian target;
- split-chain R-hat and effective sample size pass on the tested Gaussian
  target and detect intentionally disagreeing chains;
- prior predictive checks reject nonfinite, empty, and inconsistent simulator
  outputs;
- simulation-based calibration returns rank histograms and 68%/95% coverage
  summaries for an analytic calibrated scalar case;
- `paper/tables/priors.csv` exists, has ordered bounds, and all rows are marked
  `frozen_phase10`.

Current limitations:

- The internal random-walk Metropolis adapter is a validation baseline and API
  target, not the final production sampler for science campaigns.
- SBC tests are analytic one-dimensional tests. Full project SBC with the
  relativistic spectral model is still required before interpreting
  misspecified-model results.
- Phase 11 screening campaigns must not start until same-model calibration
  thresholds are documented for the final sampler/model combination.

## Independent Analytic Spectrum Cross-Validation

The Schwarzschild face-on disk spectrum has a closed form. A circular-orbit
emitter observed along the polar axis has no line-of-sight Doppler shift, so its
redshift factor is `g(r) = 1 / u^t = sqrt(1 - 3 / r)`, and the observed flux is
a redshifted, area-weighted sum of diluted blackbodies with no light bending.
`src/kerrdisk/analytic_validation.py` implements this from first principles,
independently of the production ray tracer and transfer-map assembly, and
compares it against the ray-traced backend near face-on inclination.

Outcome (production settings, `a = 0`, `i = 3 deg`, 64-equivalent full-disk
screen at `48x48`, observer at `1000 r_g`, fiducial 10 solar-mass, 8 kpc
system):

- 940 disk hits.
- Relative-L1 spectrum difference versus the analytic benchmark: `0.0409`.
- Total-flux ratio (ray-traced / analytic): `1.041`.
- Status: `PASS` at the declared `0.10` tolerance.
- Artifact: `data/processed/validation/face_on_analytic_cross_validation.csv`.

This validates the ray tracer's redshift factor, solid-angle normalization, and
flux integration against a fully independent analytic calculation. The small
residual is consistent with the finite `3 deg` inclination and light bending
that the analytic face-on limit omits.

### External ray-tracer status

External agreement against a third-party ray tracer (`GYOTO`, `RAPTOR`,
`grtrans`, `kgeo`) remains separate from the analytic cross-validation above.
`kgeo` is distributed only from its source repository and is not installable
from the package index; installing and running it requires an explicit,
user-approved dependency step. Until such a backend is installed and adapted
with documented screen-coordinate, redshift, disk-hit, and emission-angle
conventions, the external-code comparison stays `SKIPPED` while the independent
analytic cross-validation above is `PASS`.

## Phase 12 / 13.5 v5 Joint Marginalized Campaign

The confirmatory and multi-epoch campaigns were rerun with the corrected v5
pipeline and a joint marginalized fit that replaces the one-dimensional
spin-grid scan. For each condition a (spin, color-correction) spectral emulator
is built once from full-disk ray-traced transfer maps, and every replicate is
fitted with the affine-invariant ensemble sampler; the spin posterior is the
color-marginalized marginal.

Run settings (reduced-resolution first v5 run):

- 48 locked conditions, 30 replicates per condition.
- 24x24 full-disk screen (relative-L1 ~1.9 percent versus the 112x112 reference,
  below the 3 percent Gaussian noise), observer at `1000 r_g`, fiducial
  ten-solar-mass black hole at 8 kpc.
- Emulator nodes: 9 spin, 5 color-correction; emcee with 16 walkers, 350 draws,
  200 burn-in.
- Runners: `scripts/run_joint_campaign.py`, `scripts/run_joint_multi_epoch.py`;
  config `configs/production/phase12_joint_v5.yaml`.

Outcome:

- Confirmatory: 48/48 conditions completed. Under color-correction
  marginalization the condition-mean spin bias is modest (range about `-0.26`
  to `+0.41`, mean `~0.04`), far smaller than the earlier one-dimensional-fit
  values that reached `~1.1`. The inner-stress-misspecified conditions
  (`Delta_eta = 0.02`) remain biased with elevated `chi2/dof` (about `6-7`),
  while zero-stress conditions with only color-correction offset recover spin
  with wider intervals and better coverage.
- Multi-epoch: 24/24 groups completed; joint two-epoch fitting reduced the mean
  68 percent spin-interval width in 12 of 24 groups, and the change in spin bias
  is condition-dependent.

Limitations:

- This is a reduced-resolution first v5 run. Same-model coverage is degraded
  both by genuine misspecification and by the coarse emulator/screen resolution;
  the full-resolution campaign (64x64 screen, denser emulator nodes, and more
  replicates) is a separate compute job.
