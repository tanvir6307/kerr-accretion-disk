\title{KerrDisk-UQ: Reproducible Ray-Traced Thin-Disk Spectra and Multi-Epoch Spin-Bias Experiments}

\author{Tanvir Hassan}
\affiliation{Independent researcher}
\email{tanvir6307@gmail.com}

\date{June 19, 2026}

\begin{abstract}
We present KerrDisk-UQ, a reproducible Python framework for detector-independent
synthetic experiments with ray-traced Kerr thin-disk spectra. The calculation
combines registered Kerr geodesic and circular-orbit formulae, Page-Thorne
surface flux, a controlled Agol-Krolik inner-edge stress extension, diluted
blackbody emission, image-plane transfer maps, and deterministic likelihood
experiments. The current confirmatory campaign uses 48 locked synthetic
conditions, 4800 completed base replicates, 24 energy bins, 5x5 transfer maps,
and an 81/121 point spin-grid resolution comparison. All recorded independent
validation rows pass their declared tolerances, and all 48 confirmatory
conditions pass the predeclared spin-grid stability tolerance. In the
misspecified fits, condition-averaged spin biases are large in several parts of
the grid, reaching 1.1323 in the table aggregated by spin and inclination. A
fixed-luminosity two-epoch shared-spin comparison reduces the mean 68 percent
spin-interval width in 15 of 24 groups, but reduces the mean absolute spin bias
in only 3 of 24 groups. These results support a limited conclusion: in this
idealized detector-independent experiment, multi-epoch sharing can reduce
random interval width without automatically removing systematic spin bias. The
present release does not establish agreement with an external ray-tracing code,
does not fold detector responses, and does not include iterative returning
radiation, photon-capture corrections in the spectra, GRMHD evolution,
reflection, Comptonization, or observational data.
\end{abstract}

\keywords{computational physics, black holes, accretion disks, ray tracing, uncertainty quantification}

\section{Introduction}

Thermal continuum fitting connects the observed temperature and normalization
of an optically thick disk spectrum to the radius of the inner emitting region,
and hence to the Kerr spin when the disk is assumed to terminate at the
innermost stable circular orbit (ISCO). This inference chain depends on
relativistic disk structure, spectral hardening, transfer through the curved
spacetime, and assumptions about stresses near the inner edge
\cite{PageThorne1974,LiZimmermanNarayanMcClintock2005,DavisBlaesHubenyTurner2005,DavisHubeny2006,McClintockNarayanSteiner2014}.
The same ingredients also create a natural setting for computational
uncertainty-quantification experiments: controlled synthetic spectra can be
generated under one model and analyzed under another to isolate when an
apparently precise spin estimate is biased.

Prior work supplies the relevant physical and numerical foundations. Kerr
orbits and ISCO quantities are analytic in Boyer-Lindquist coordinates
\cite{BardeenPressTeukolsky1972}. Page and Thorne give the steady thin-disk
flux formalism used here \cite{PageThorne1974}. Cunningham introduced
relativistic transfer calculations for disk spectra \cite{Cunningham1975}.
Agol and Krolik provide a controlled prescription for a nonzero inner-edge
stress term \cite{AgolKrolik2000}. Detailed atmosphere and continuum-fitting
models such as KERRBB and BHSPEC motivate the color-correction sensitivity
studied here \cite{LiZimmermanNarayanMcClintock2005,DavisHubeny2006}.
GRMHD-based tests and recent continuum-model studies emphasize that deviations
from idealized thin-disk assumptions can affect spin inference
\cite{KulkarniPennaShafee2011,PennaMcKinneyNarayan2010,SalvesenMiller2021,MummeryIngramDavisFabian2024,MummeryEtAl2025RapidOrPlunging}.

This paper does not analyze observational data. It asks a narrower
computational question: in a validated, detector-independent Kerr thin-disk
synthetic campaign, how do controlled spectral-hardening and weak inner-stress
misspecification affect recovered spin, and does a two-epoch shared-spin fit
reduce interval width or bias relative to separate single-epoch fits? The
answer is derived only from versioned repository outputs and the claim audit in
\texttt{paper/claim\_audit.md}.

\section{Physical model}

All production physical formulae are registered in
\texttt{docs/equation\_registry.md}. Internally the code uses geometric units
with \(G=M=c=1\), Boyer-Lindquist coordinates \((t,r,\theta,\phi)\), and signed
dimensionless spin \(a_\ast\), where positive spin denotes a prograde disk.
The Kerr metric, event horizon, equatorial circular-orbit quantities, and ISCO
radius follow Bardeen, Press, and Teukolsky \cite{BardeenPressTeukolsky1972}.

The baseline disk is geometrically thin, optically thick, equatorial, and
time-steady. The zero-torque surface flux follows the Page-Thorne
conservation-law form \cite{PageThorne1974}. In the code convention, the
one-face local flux is
\begin{equation}
F(r)=
\frac{\dot M}{4\pi \sqrt{-g}_{\rm disk}}
\frac{-\Omega_{,r}}{(E-\Omega L)^2}
\int_{r_{\rm in}}^r
(E-\Omega L)L_{,r'}\,dr',
\end{equation}
with \(r_{\rm in}=r_{\rm ISCO}\), \(\sqrt{-g}_{\rm disk}=r\) for the adopted
Page-Thorne disk-area convention, and \(E\), \(L\), and \(\Omega\) evaluated for
equatorial circular orbits. A controlled nonzero-stress extension adds the
Agol-Krolik flux term parameterized by an efficiency increment
\(\Delta\eta\) \cite{AgolKrolik2000}. The implemented stress term is confined
to the disk outside the ISCO and is not a plunging-region model.

The local emission model is a diluted blackbody,
\begin{equation}
I_{\nu,{\rm em}} =
\frac{1}{f_{\rm col}^4}
B_\nu(f_{\rm col}T_{\rm eff}),
\end{equation}
with \(T_{\rm eff}=(F/\sigma_{\rm SB})^{1/4}\). The Phase 12 v4 confirmatory
campaign uses physical emission angles and the normalized electron-scattering
limb-darkening law recorded in the equation registry. Color correction is
treated as a sensitivity parameter, not as a claim of atmosphere realism.

\section{Numerical methods}

Null rays are initialized on a local observer screen and evolved through a
scalar Hamiltonian Kerr ray tracer. Disk-hit records are converted into
transfer-map samples containing screen coordinates, solid angle, emission
radius and azimuth, redshift factor, physical emission angle, Hamiltonian
diagnostic, and step count. The observed specific intensity uses the invariant
\(I_\nu/\nu^3\), and detector-independent binned spectra are constructed from
the ray-traced transfer records.

The confirmatory campaign used the configuration
\texttt{phase12\_ray\_traced\_transfer\_v4}. Table~\ref{tab:design}
summarizes the versioned design. It contains 48 locked conditions and 4800
completed base replicates. Each spectrum uses 24 energy bins and a 5x5
cell-centered image-plane transfer map. The base spin grid has 81 points and
the stability rerun uses 121 points.

likelihood and failure accounting are deterministic under the stored seeds and
configuration files.

\section{Experiment design}

The confirmatory grid was generated from a locked condition list and analyzed
after the analysis-freeze file was written. The fitted quantity emphasized here
is spin bias, defined as posterior-mean spin minus injected spin. The reported
coverage is the empirical inclusion fraction of the injected spin in the
stored 68 percent or 95 percent interval over deterministic noise replicates.
The design intentionally tests misspecification: spectra generated with
controlled color-correction and stress assumptions are fitted with simpler
model assumptions.

The Phase 13.5 comparison pairs conditions that share spin, inclination, true
and fitted color-correction assumptions, and stress setting. The two selected
luminosities are treated as fixed epoch metadata. The joint fit shares one spin
parameter across two epochs; it does not fit luminosity, mass, distance, or
inclination as nuisance parameters.

\subsection{Two-epoch shared-spin comparison}

The two-epoch comparison is condition-dependent rather than uniformly
beneficial. Of 24 completed two-epoch groups, 15 have a smaller mean 68 percent
spin-interval width under joint fitting than under separate single-epoch
fitting. The mean width decreases from 0.0199200 to 0.0183144, corresponding to
a mean width-reduction fraction of 0.0678137. Bias does not improve in the same
way: only 3 of 24 groups have lower mean absolute bias in the joint fit, and
the mean joint absolute bias exceeds the separate-epoch value by 0.00264103.
Figure~\ref{fig:multi} shows this contrast.

\section{Discussion}

The computational result is qualitatively consistent with the structure of the
problem. Shared-parameter fitting can reduce random uncertainty when two
epochs constrain the same spin, but a shared incorrect model can also share the
same systematic error. The Phase 13.5 result therefore supports a limited
statement: multi-epoch fitting is useful as an uncertainty-control strategy
only when the model space contains the relevant spectral-hardening and
inner-edge physics, or when the misspecification is otherwise diagnosed.

The present implementation is closer to a reproducible numerical experiment
than to a production observational pipeline. Its strengths are versioned
configuration files, deterministic seeds, machine-readable outputs, analytic
and independent validation rows, convergence artifacts, and a claim audit. Its
limitations are equally important. The spectra are detector-independent. The
ray tracer is the internal scalar reference implementation and lacks an
accepted external-code agreement artifact. The 5x5 image-plane maps are
validated for spectra in selected benchmark cases but are not high-resolution
images. Returning-radiation and photon-capture outcomes are recorded only as
diagnostics. The stress extension is the controlled Agol-Krolik outside-ISCO
term, not a GRMHD or plunging-region emission model.

For APL Computational Physics, the main contribution is the reproducible
computational workflow and the claim-limited uncertainty experiment. Before
submission, the remaining bibliography rows marked \texttt{NEEDS\_REVIEW} in
\texttt{literature/reference\_verification.csv} should be checked against ADS,
arXiv, or publisher records, and an external ray-tracing comparison should be
added if external agreement is to be claimed.

\section{Conclusions}

KerrDisk-UQ currently supports the following conclusions.
First, the Phase 12 v4 confirmatory campaign completed 48 locked conditions and
4800 base replicates with a ray-traced transfer-map backend and
electron-scattering limb darkening. Second, all recorded independent validation
rows pass, and all 48 confirmatory conditions pass the declared spin-grid
stability tolerance. Third, misspecified synthetic fits can return large spin
biases and poor coverage in this detector-independent campaign. Fourth,
two-epoch shared-spin fitting reduces mean interval width in many groups but
does not generally reduce systematic spin bias in the current fixed-luminosity
comparison.

The work does not support claims about observational spin errors, external
ray-tracer agreement, detector-calibrated spectra, high-resolution image
morphology, GRMHD disks, or spectra corrected for returning radiation or photon
capture. Those are explicit targets for future validation and modeling work.
